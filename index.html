snapshot.

photos and writing by Andrea Hefferan

school walks me down the plank

can’t look back, only down

into the water’s baby blues

a deadly relief from the swashbuckling executioner

May 27th swooped down to catch my body mid-jump

Wendy Darling can’t die in this fairy tale

Safe in summer’s arms until the day she has to grow up

face Hook and his crew once more

but for now, summer whispers in my ear,

“you’re safe here”
My head is dizzy

A buzz from being up all night

A simulated daytime when really it’s 2am

The clock says 9, my brain says 2

I’m high off the daylight that should have left hours ago

Our plane fighting time and winning

Inspiration comes in fleeting glances

Better catch it before it’s gone

Bursts of thoughts amid exhausted adrenaline

Phone is going to die. So am I.
An inexorable love affair

irresistible pleasure at consumption

consuming guilt in the aftermath

willpower too weak to stay away

taste dissipates and fat accumulates

Yet I always come back for more

can’t stop, won’t stop.

but stopping is what I want when my body stretches.

my clothes and the mirror scream that I’m fat

but I step away and forget myself

I fall back into food’s toxic embrace
